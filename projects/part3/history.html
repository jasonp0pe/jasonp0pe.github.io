History of Chess

Chess is believed to have originated in India in the 6th century, where it was known as chaturanga. It eventually spread to Persia, where it was called shatranj, and later to Europe during the Middle Ages. Over the centuries, chess evolved into the modern game we know today.

6th Century: Origin in India (Chaturanga)

The game began as chaturanga in India, representing four military divisions: infantry, cavalry, elephantry, and chariotry. It was played on an 8x8 grid and laid the foundation for modern chess.

7th Century: Spread to Persia (Shatranj)

As the game spread to Persia, it evolved into shatranj, where the pieces took on their familiar forms: the king, queen, rooks, knights, bishops, and pawns. It became a popular game in the Islamic world.

15th Century: Modern Chess in Europe

During the Middle Ages, chess spread to Europe, where it further evolved. The rules were formalized, and by the 15th century, chess became the modern game we know today, with the queen becoming the most powerful piece.
